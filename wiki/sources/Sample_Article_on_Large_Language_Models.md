# Sample Article on Large Language Models

This page summarizes the key points from the sample article on Large Language Models (LLMs).

## Overview
Large Language Models (LLMs) are artificial intelligence programs capable of recognizing and generating text. They are trained on vast text datasets and can perform various natural language processing (NLP) tasks, including translation, summarization, and question answering.

## Key Researcher
- [[Andrej Karpathy]]: Noted for work at OpenAI and Tesla; has discussed the concept of an "LLM Wiki" as a persistent knowledge base.

## How LLMs Work
LLMs typically use transformer architectures, which process words in relation to all others in a sentence. The attention mechanism enables context understanding and coherent text generation.

## Applications
- Content generation
- [[Chatbots]] and virtual assistants
- Code generation and explanation
- Data analysis and summarization

## Challenges
Despite their capabilities, LLMs face challenges:
- [[Hallucinations]] (generating factually incorrect information)
- Bias from training data
- Computational cost
- Ethical considerations
